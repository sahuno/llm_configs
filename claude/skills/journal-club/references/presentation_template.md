# Canonical Journal Club Presentation Template

The default skeleton used by Stages 4 and 5. Adapt percentages by audience
and time, but keep the section order — it's the natural narrative arc.

## Sections

1. **Title slide** — paper, authors, you, date
2. **Why this paper** (1–2 min) — the hook. Citation impact, controversy, or
   relevance to the group.
3. **Background** (3–5 min) — just enough field context for the audience to
   follow. Don't over-teach.
4. **Central question / hypothesis** (1 min) — one sentence. The whole talk
   hangs on this.
5. **Approach (methods)** (3–5 min) — focus on novel methods, not standard
   ones. Show the study design as a diagram.
6. **Key results — figure walk** (8–15 min) — 3–5 main figures. Each gets:
   - What they did (one phrase)
   - What they found (one phrase, with effect size)
   - Why it matters (one phrase)
7. **Critical evaluation** (3–5 min) — strengths and 2–3 defensible
   weaknesses.
8. **Implications & open questions** (2–3 min) — what this changes, what's
   still unknown, what you'd do next.
9. **Discussion prompts** (2 min) — 2–3 seed questions for the room.

## Defaults

| Time budget | Slides | Figures | Background depth |
|---|---|---|---|
| 15 min | ~15 | 2–3 | Light |
| 25 min | ~25 | 3–5 | Standard |
| 45 min | ~40 | 4–6 | Deep |

## Audience adjustments

- **Specialist (same field)**: cut Background to 1 slide, expand Methods and
  Critical evaluation
- **Mixed (department-wide)**: expand Background and Glossary, keep Methods
  light, focus Figure walk on intuition not technicalities
- **Clinical (translational focus)**: expand Implications, light on
  bench-method details
- **Computational / ML focus**: expand Methods (especially novel pipelines),
  add benchmarks/comparisons, light on biology background

## Quality bar

- One slide, one idea
- Figures are large (≥40% of slide area)
- Speaker notes exist for every slide
- A non-specialist could follow the first 5 minutes
- Every claim traces back to a figure, table, or methods detail
